Can you identify the end of a coin’s rally using a combination of on-chain, social and trading data?

The answer is yes - and it’s easier than you might think.

Volume is a powerful concept in finance, often corresponding with major shifts in market conditions.

On Sanbase, we calculate not one, but three ‘volumes’ for every coin:

1. Trading volume (based on speculative activity on exchanges)
2. Social volume (amount of coin mentions on crypto social media)
3. On-chain volume (amount of daily addresses interacting with the coin)

Although these are entirely different data points, the same top formation often appears in all three indicators.

As long as the price keeps growing on rising volume, the trend is likely to continue. Once we see a clear divergence between the price and volume, the top might be near.

Ethereum’s latest drop is a perfect example - all three volumes dipped simultaneously right before the correction, as the price was still pushing upward.
