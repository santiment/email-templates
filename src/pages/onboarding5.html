1. Circulation

Why It Matters:

Circulation measures the transfer of unique tokens between addresses, shining a light on actual asset movement and network utility.

Key Takeaways:

  • Unique Measurement: Focuses on the movement of distinct tokens rather than total transaction volume.
  • Price Indicators: Spikes in circulation often signal impending price changes, either as an increase or a local top.

2. Daily Active Addresses (DAA)

Why It Matters:

DAA measures the number of unique active addresses involved in transactions on a blockchain network each day. This metric is a powerful indicator of user activity and engagement, often correlating with price movements.

Key Takeaways:

  • Positive Correlation with Price: Increased DAA often signals heightened interest and potential price movement. For example, Ethereum has shown positive price trends during periods of rising DAA.
  • Market Top Indicators: A sudden spike might suggest a market top, potentially signalling a forthcoming sell-off.

To get the most out of the DAA metric, consider using it alongside others like Whale Transactions for a broader perspective. Interested in seeing the data visualized? 3. Trading and Transaction Volume

Why It Matters:

These metrics reveal the level of activity and liquidity in the market, providing insight into market sentiment and potential price changes.

Key Takeaways:

  • Trading Volume: Includes only trade-related transactions both on-chain and on centralized exchanges.
  • Transaction Volume: Encompasses all token movements on-chain, indicating real-world utility and network usage.

When both the price of a token and the trading volume are moving in the same direction, it generally suggests a bullish market consensus.If both the price and trading volume are decreasing, it generally indicates a bearish market sentiment.
