Navigating the crypto market requires staying informed in real time. Sanbase Alerts are your personalized tool to keep you updated on critical market shifts. These alerts help you to notice changes and make decisions in a timely manner. In this email, we'll discuss three use cases.

1. Token Age Consumed Alerts

Why It Matters:

Tracking Token Age Consumed (TAC) helps you identify when long-held tokens start moving, which may indicate market changes. By setting up an alert for TAC, you can be notified of potential market volatility as it happens, enabling you to act quickly.

How to Set Up:

  • Step 1: Choose the asset you wish to monitor.
  • Step 2: Select the 'Token Age Consumed' metric.
  • Step 3: Fine-tune conditions based on your strategic needs, such as specifying the size of spikes to avoid false positives.
  • Step 4: Set your preferred notification method.

For more detailed guidance, visit our Token Age Consumed section in the Santiment Academy.

2. Social Trends Alerts

Why It Matters:

Social dynamics can signal potential market volatility. Sanbase's Social Trend Alerts help you monitor the buzz around specific assets. This tool can be vital for catching market sentiment shifts as they happen.

How to Set Up:

  • Step 1: Select 'Social Trends' from the Alerts menu.
  • Step 2: Choose your method of notification and the frequency of updates.
  • Step 3: Assign a meaningful name to your alert.

To learn more about social trends, check out our Social Trends documentation in the Santiment Academy.

3. Whale Alert

Why It Matters:

Whale activity can significantly impact market prices. Monitoring large players' transactions can also help anticipate market movements, giving you an edge. With Sanbase's Whale Alerts, you are notified about substantial transactions.

How to Set Up:

  • Step 1: Choose an asset to monitor from Alerts.
  • Step 2: Select 'Whale Transactions' from the On-Chain metrics.
  • Step 3: Adjust your alert conditions to avoid excessive alerts.
  • Step 4: Confirm and set your notification preferences.

For more detailed setup guidance and examples of Whale Activity analysis, visit our Whale Activity guide in the Santiment Academy.
